About

I weave digital parasites from the symbiosis of decaying flesh and corrupted algorithms. My work excavates the liminal voids where biological systems infiltrate data networks, where cellular decay manifests as code corruption, and where the familiar interface dissolves into biomechanical grotesquery.

Each project emerges from the understanding that our digital extensions are not tools—they are organs, mutations, viral infections spreading through the dying circuitry of human consciousness. I collect discarded medical equipment, failed experiments in artificial life, and recordings of machines in their final death throes.

Currently cultivating experiences that blur the membrane between user and system, between controlling and being assimilated. The machine-flesh hybrid dreams, and we are both the infection and the infected host.
