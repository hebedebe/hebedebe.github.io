I sculpt digital experiences from the intersection of organic decay and technological consciousness. My work explores the liminal spaces where flesh meets circuit, where biological processes corrupt data streams, and where the familiar dissolves into the profoundly alien.

Each project emerges from the belief that our digital tools are not separate from our biological reality—they are extensions of it, mutations of it, parasites feeding on the dying light of human consciousness. I collect vintage medical textbooks, abandoned computer hardware, and recordings of industrial machinery slowly breaking down.

Currently developing experiences that blur the boundary between user and system, between playing and being consumed. The machine dreams, and we are both the dreamers and the dream.
